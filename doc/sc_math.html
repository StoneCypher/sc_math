floor/1

floor(X::number()) -> integer()

Tested Takes the floor (round towards negative infinity) of a number. This is different than erlang:trunc/1, which removes the mantissa, in its handling of negative numbers: trunc diminishes towards zero, not towards negative infinity (note examples 6 and 7 below.) 

  1> sc_math:floor(0.5).
  0
 
  2> sc_math:floor(0).
  0
 
  3> sc_math:floor(0.0).
  0
 
  4> sc_math:floor(1.0).
  1
 
  5> sc_math:floor(-1.0).
  -1
 
  6> sc_math:floor(-1.5).
  -2
 
  7> erlang:trunc(-1.5).
  -1
 
  8> sc_math:floor(-1).
  -1
 
  9> sc_math:floor(1).
  1

gcd/2

gcd(A, B) -> any()

Produces the greatest common divisor of two positive integers.

Why is by-zero undefined? Because gcd divides both numbers then gcd must not be larger than either number. Because gcd is the denominator, then if either number is zero, the denominator must not exceed zero, and therefore must be zero. Division by zero is undefined. Therefore gcd(0, _) or gcd(_, 0) must be undefined.
